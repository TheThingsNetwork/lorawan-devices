CO₂ sensing reports the concentration of carbon dioxide in the surrounding air, usually expressed in parts per million (ppm). Because CO₂ levels climb when people exhale in an enclosed space, the reading is a reliable proxy for ventilation adequacy and room occupancy, and a core input for indoor air quality programs.

LoRaWAN CO₂ devices typically pair a non-dispersive infrared (NDIR) sensing element with a low-power radio. They sample at set intervals, encode the value into a small uplink payload, and send it over long range to a gateway. A payload codec on the network server decodes the data for dashboards, alerts and automated ventilation control. Many models also report temperature and humidity in the same message.

Common deployments include schools, offices, meeting rooms, hospitals, greenhouses and demand-controlled ventilation systems. When comparing devices, weigh:

- Measurement range, accuracy and sensing technology
- Reporting interval, power source and battery life
- Enclosure rating and supported regional frequency plans
- Availability of a documented payload decoder
