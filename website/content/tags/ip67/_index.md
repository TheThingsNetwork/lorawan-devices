**IP67** is an IEC 60529 ingress-protection rating. The two digits describe two separate things. The first digit, **6**, is the highest solids rating: the enclosure is fully dust-tight, with no ingress of dust permitted. The second digit, **7**, describes liquid protection: the device withstands temporary immersion in water, generally to a depth of 1 meter for up to 30 minutes.

That combination makes IP67 well suited to harsh deployments:

- Outdoor and exposed sites facing rain, dirt, and wind-blown dust
- Dusty industrial and agricultural settings
- Washdown areas where equipment is hose-cleaned
- Locations risking brief, accidental immersion

Note that IP67 covers temporary immersion, not continuous submersion or high-pressure jets. For sustained underwater use, IP68 is the relevant rating.

Enclosure rating matters because a LoRaWAN sensor often runs unattended for years in the field. Choosing an IP67 device helps ensure dust and water do not reach the electronics, protecting battery life, radio performance, and long-term reliability.
