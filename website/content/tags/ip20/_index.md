Under IEC 60529, **IP20** decodes digit by digit. The first digit, **2**, rates protection against solids: the enclosure keeps out objects 12.5 mm in diameter or larger, including fingers, preventing accidental contact with live or moving internal parts. The second digit, **0**, rates protection against liquids: there is **none**. IP20 devices are not splash-, drip-, or waterproof and offer no protection against dust.

This makes IP20 a strictly **indoor, dry-environment** rating. It suits sensors and gateways installed in offices, homes, server rooms, and protected cabinets where conditions stay clean and controlled.

When choosing a LoRaWAN device, the enclosure rating determines where it can safely live:

- **Indoor, clean and dry:** IP20 is appropriate.
- **Dusty, humid, washdown, or outdoor:** step up to a sealed rating such as IP65 or higher.

Matching the IP rating to the deployment site protects both the electronics and the people near them, and avoids early failures from moisture or contamination.
