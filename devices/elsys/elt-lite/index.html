About this device

The ELSYS ELT Lite is a general LoRaWAN® device for measuring analog or digital signals. It is a 1-channel GPIO wireless transceiver and can also provide power to external sensors. ELT Lite is suitable for applications such as smart cities, agriculture, and distance measuring.

The ELT Lite an easy but powerful LoRAWAN device for measuring analog or digital signals. supports 4 regional frequency plans and operates as a Class A LoRaWAN device. A payload codec is published in the repository, so uplinks are decoded automatically when you onboard it to The Things Stack.

Key features

  • Sensors: digital input, analog input, pulse count.
  • LoRa Alliance certified, verified interoperability with The Things Stack and other LoRaWAN network servers.
  • Payload codec included, uplink decoder ready for one-click activation.
  • 4 frequency plans supported: AS923, AU915-928, EU863-870, US902-928.
  • Battery powered (AA), user replaceable.

Manual setup

1
Create an application

In The Things Stack console, go to Applications → Create application and pick a unique application ID.

2
Register an end device

Choose From the LoRaWAN Device Repository and search for “ELT Lite an easy but powerful LoRAWAN device for measuring analog or digital signals.”. Select your firmware version and frequency plan.

3
Enter device credentials

Provide the DevEUI, JoinEUI and AppKey printed on the device label or QR code.

4
Power on & verify uplinks

Within a few minutes you'll see the join request and decoded uplinks in the Live data tab.
