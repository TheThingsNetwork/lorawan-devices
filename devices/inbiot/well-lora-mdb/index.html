About this device

MICA WELL Modbus is a compact LoRaWAN Indoor Air Quality Sensor that monitors temperature, humidity, CO2, PMs, Formaldehyde, Ozone, No2, CO and Noise.

The MICA WELL Modbus - Indoor Air Quality Sensor supports 5 regional frequency plans and operates as a Class A/C LoRaWAN device. A payload codec is published in the repository, so uplinks are decoded automatically when you onboard it to The Things Stack.

Key features

  • Sensors: temperature, humidity, co2, tvoc, pm2.5, pm10, sound, no2, o3, co.
  • LoRa Alliance certified, verified interoperability with The Things Stack and other LoRaWAN network servers.
  • Payload codec included, uplink decoder ready for one-click activation.
  • 5 frequency plans supported: AS923, AU915-928, EU863-870, KR920-923, US902-928.

Manual setup

In The Things Stack console, go to Applications → Create application and pick a unique application ID.

2
Register an end device

Choose From the LoRaWAN Device Repository and search for “MICA WELL Modbus - Indoor Air Quality Sensor”. Select your firmware version and frequency plan.

3
Enter device credentials

Provide the DevEUI, JoinEUI and AppKey printed on the device label or QR code.

4
Power on & verify uplinks

Within a few minutes you'll see the join request and decoded uplinks in the Live data tab.
