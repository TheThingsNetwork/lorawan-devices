IP30-Rated LoRaWAN Devices

IP30-rated LoRaWAN devices keep out solid objects 2.5 mm and larger but offer no protection against water, making them suited to clean, dry indoor use.

An IP30 rating is defined by IEC 60529. The first digit, 3, means the enclosure is protected against solid foreign objects 2.5 mm and larger, such as tools and thick wires, though fine dust can still enter. The second digit, 0, means there is no protection against liquids of any kind.

In practice, IP30 LoRaWAN devices are built for clean, dry, indoor environments. They are a good match for offices, server and control rooms, retail spaces, and sheltered enclosures where the main concern is incidental contact rather than the elements.

IP30 is not suitable for:

  • Outdoor or unsheltered installations
  • Dusty industrial floors or agricultural sites
  • Washdown areas or anywhere exposed to splashes, jets, or condensation

Enclosure rating matters because it determines where a device can survive long term. Matching the IP code to the deployment site prevents premature failure, so confirm the environment before selecting an IP30 device over a dust- or water-resistant alternative.

Frequently asked questions

Is IP30 waterproof?

No. The second digit of IP30 is 0, meaning the enclosure has no rated protection against dripping water, spray, jets, or immersion. Keep IP30 devices dry.

What does IP30 protect against?

The first digit 3 means the enclosure blocks solid foreign objects 2.5 mm and larger, such as tools and thick wires. It does not keep out fine dust.

Where should I deploy IP30 LoRaWAN devices?

IP30 suits clean, dry indoor settings like offices, control rooms, or sheltered cabinets. For dust, moisture, or outdoor exposure, choose a higher-rated enclosure.
