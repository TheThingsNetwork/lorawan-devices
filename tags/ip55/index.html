IP55-Rated LoRaWAN Devices

IP55 LoRaWAN devices are dust-protected and resist low-pressure water jets from any direction, suiting outdoor and weather-exposed installations.

Under IEC 60529, IP55 breaks down into two digits. The first digit, 5, means the enclosure is dust-protected: dust ingress is not completely prevented, but any dust that enters is limited and cannot interfere with the device’s operation. The second digit, 5, means it is protected against water jets projected from a nozzle in any direction at low pressure, with no harmful effects.

That combination makes IP55 a practical, widely used rating for LoRaWAN sensors and gateways installed outdoors or in demanding indoor settings:

  • Outdoor mounting exposed to rain, spray, and windblown dust
  • Agricultural, industrial, and utility environments
  • Light hose-down or cleaning, but not immersion

Enclosure rating matters because it defines where hardware can survive long-term. An under-rated device may fail early from moisture or dust, while over-specifying adds cost and bulk. IP55 strikes a common middle ground: robust against weather and jets, but not sealed for submersion. If your site involves immersion or powerful pressure washing, step up to IP67, IP68, or IP69K instead.

Frequently asked questions

What does IP55 mean?

Under IEC 60529, the first digit 5 means the enclosure is dust-protected: dust ingress is not fully prevented, but any that enters cannot interfere with safe operation. The second digit 5 means it resists water projected as low-pressure jets from any direction with no harmful effects.

Is IP55 waterproof?

No. IP55 is water-jet resistant, not waterproof. It withstands low-pressure jets and rain from any direction, but it is not rated for immersion. For temporary or continuous submersion you need IP67 or IP68.

Is IP55 fully dust-tight?

No. The first digit 5 is dust-protected, not dust-tight. Some dust may enter but cannot impair operation. For a fully dust-tight seal, look for a first digit of 6, as in IP65 or IP66.
