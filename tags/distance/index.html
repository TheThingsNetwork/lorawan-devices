LoRaWAN Distance Sensors

LoRaWAN distance sensors measure the range to a surface or object and report it wirelessly over long-range, low-power networks.

Distance sensing measures the range between the device and a target surface or object, usually in centimeters or millimeters. LoRaWAN distance sensors commonly use ultrasonic echo timing, optical time-of-flight (laser), or radar to determine that range, then send the value as an uplink over a LoRaWAN network. Low duty cycles and battery operation let many devices run for years on infrequent reports.

These devices appear across industries: utilities and waste management monitor tank, silo, and bin fill levels; smart cities track parking and bay occupancy; facilities watch gate and door positions; and environmental teams measure snow depth or water clearance. Because they report distance to a surface, many models also serve as level sensors.

When comparing options, weigh:

  • Measurement range, accuracy, and sensing technology (ultrasonic, laser, radar)
  • Beam angle and minimum detection distance
  • Power source, battery life, enclosure (IP) rating, supported frequency plans, and an available payload decoder

Match the sensing method and enclosure to your target material and mounting environment for reliable readings.

Frequently asked questions

How does a LoRaWAN distance sensor work?

It measures the range to a target, typically using ultrasonic, time-of-flight (laser/optical), or radar techniques, then transmits the reading over a LoRaWAN network to a gateway and application server. Battery operation and infrequent uplinks let a single device run for years.

What can LoRaWAN distance sensors be used for?

Common uses include tank and bin fill-level monitoring, parking and bay occupancy, gate or door position, snow depth, and clearance detection. Because they report range to a surface, many distance sensors double as level sensors.

What should I compare when choosing a distance sensor?

Look at measurement range and accuracy, sensing technology and beam angle, power source and battery life, IP enclosure rating for outdoor or wet sites, supported regional frequency plans, and whether a payload decoder is provided.
