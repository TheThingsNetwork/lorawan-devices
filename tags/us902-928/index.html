LoRaWAN US902-928 Frequency Plan

US902-928 is the LoRaWAN regional plan for the 902-928 MHz ISM band used across the United States, Canada, and much of the Americas.

US902-928 is the LoRaWAN regional frequency plan covering the 902-928 MHz sub-GHz ISM band. It is defined for ITU Region 2 and is the standard plan across North America, including the United States, Canada, and Mexico, as well as many other countries throughout the Americas that permit operation in this band.

LoRaWAN devices communicate only on the channels and data rates defined by their regional parameters. Radio regulations differ by country, so a device built for one plan will not operate correctly under another. To join a network and exchange data with nearby gateways, an end device must support the frequency plan used where it is deployed.

When sourcing hardware for a US902-928 deployment, confirm that each device:

  • Explicitly lists US902-928 (sometimes shown as US915) support
  • Targets the country or market where it will operate

The devices listed on this page are catalogued as compatible with the US902-928 frequency plan, helping integrators shortlist hardware for deployments across the Americas.

Frequently asked questions

Where is the US902-928 frequency plan used?

US902-928 is the LoRaWAN regional plan for ITU Region 2. It is widely used in the United States, Canada, Mexico, and many other countries across the Americas that allow operation in the 902-928 MHz ISM band.

What frequency range does US902-928 cover?

As the name indicates, the plan operates in the sub-GHz band spanning roughly 902 MHz to 928 MHz. Uplink and downlink channels, along with default data rates, follow the LoRaWAN Regional Parameters for this band.

Why must a device support the right frequency plan?

LoRaWAN devices transmit only on the channels and frequencies defined by their regional plan. A device must support the plan used where it is deployed to lawfully and reliably join a network and exchange data with gateways.
