LoRaWAN pH Sensors

LoRaWAN pH sensors continuously measure the acidity or alkalinity of water and soil and report readings wirelessly over long distances.

pH sensing measures how acidic or alkaline a solution is on a scale of 0 to 14, a critical indicator in water quality, agriculture and process monitoring. A LoRaWAN pH sensor pairs a pH electrode (often with built-in temperature compensation) to a low-power radio that wakes on a schedule, samples the probe, and sends a small encoded payload uplink to a LoRaWAN gateway. The long range and low power of LoRaWAN suit remote tanks, ponds, fields and treatment sites where wiring and cellular coverage are impractical.

Common applications include:

  • Surface and ground water quality monitoring
  • Aquaculture and fish-farm tanks
  • Hydroponics, soil and irrigation management
  • Wastewater and industrial process control

When comparing devices, check the measurement range and accuracy, automatic temperature compensation, probe type and replacement cost, enclosure and probe IP rating, supported regional frequency plans, and whether a payload decoder is provided. Calibration requirements and expected battery life under your reporting interval are also important selection factors.

Frequently asked questions

What does a LoRaWAN pH sensor measure?

It measures pH, the acidity or alkalinity of a solution on a scale of 0 to 14, typically in water or soil. The device transmits each reading wirelessly over a LoRaWAN network so you can monitor conditions remotely without wired infrastructure.

How long does the battery last on a LoRaWAN pH sensor?

Battery life depends on reporting interval, transmit power and environment, but many LoRaWAN pH sensors are designed to run for years on internal batteries by waking only to take a reading and uplink, then returning to sleep.

Do LoRaWAN pH sensors need calibration?

Yes. Like all pH electrodes, they require periodic calibration against standard buffer solutions to stay accurate, and probes are consumable and degrade over time. Check the maintenance and probe-replacement guidance for each model.
